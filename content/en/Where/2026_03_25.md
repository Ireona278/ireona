I once thought like this. It's a place similar to a wiki, or a place similar to a blog. That sort of thing. I think I just looked at the characteristics of what is roughly called a wiki and what is called a blog, and then did as I pleased. But look at this place now. Is this really a place that deserves to be called a "wiki"? Is it a place that deserves to be called a "blog"?

I looked up what other people think a wiki and a blog are, and I also checked what Gemini has to say. Looking at those expressions, what I felt first was that the reason why the expresser made such an expression is completely missing, and it feels quite convoluted—like, "it's this expression just because it is." Seeing that makes my head ache. Even as I write this, my head is starting to ache quite a bit.

I wanted to calmly unwrap why I find this so headache-inducing. However, I think I'd collapse if I tried to cross-validate every possible case, so I intend to look at how Namuwiki expresses "wiki" and "blog," and how Gemini expresses them. It's because I'm familiar with Namuwiki and Gemini.

Let's look at Namuwiki first. I'll bring some content from the "Wiki" document on Namuwiki as of the date I'm writing this.

"A wiki is a website or system where editing rights for documents are granted to everyone. This includes Wikipedia, Namuwiki, Enha-gye Wiki, and Nosmoke, which are well known to many people, and anyone can contribute to the wiki using the knowledge they possess."

This is a direct quote from the summary in the "Wiki" document on Namuwiki. This space is different from a wiki starting right here. "Editing rights for documents are granted to everyone"—I don't know the principle of how those editing rights can belong only to me at the time of expressing this, but at any rate, doesn't it seem like the editing rights belong to just one person, me? Other than that, nothing else really catches my eye right now.

Then, what kind of expression does Gemini make about a "wiki"? Since Gemini's expressions are so long, it's difficult to decide which content to bring. So, out of necessity, I'll bring an abbreviated version (the full text can be seen in my Gemini usage history).

"A place where anyone can easily edit, connect with each other, record all changes, and converse together"

About this much? I asked Gemini for the criteria to be recognized as a "wiki," and it pointed out things like these.

"
1. It is the most important criterion. Not only specific experts or managers should write; the reader must be able to become the author.
2. A wiki must be able to be edited without separate dedicated software or complex HTML coding.
3. Rather than a simple listing of documents, documents must be organically connected to one another.
4. The fact that anyone can edit also means anyone can break it. Therefore, a wiki must be capable of "time travel."
5. When a disagreement arises over the content of a document, a space is needed to lead to an agreement instead of fighting.
"
