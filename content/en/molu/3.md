March 29, 2026. This is Ireona. I want to express the various frustrations I am feeling today as a result of a day trip.

Regarding the date of this writing, something happened yesterday around 4:18 AM—the crack of dawn. I was fast asleep when a sudden, incredibly loud song jolted me awake. It didn't sound like someone singing live; it felt like a recorded track played at an extreme volume, much like an election campaign truck. Along with the music, I heard a rhythmic honking—something like "pa-ra-ba-ra-ba-ra-bam." It didn't last long; it was short and intense. I suspect it was a group of motorcycle gangs (bikers), the kind that roam around the neighborhood causing a disturbance. That’s what happened.

Aside from such one-off events, there is this ongoing issue. What we call "fine dust" seems to have stayed at a "Bad" level for a very long time. The high concentration has persisted for quite a while. Even now, the fine dust (PM10) is 65μg/m³ and the ultrafine dust (PM2.5) is 43μg/m³. At a rough guess, these levels have been maintained for over a week. Even on the morning I woke up to those biker sounds, the dust concentration was that high. I thought to myself, "What a perfect morning."

From my current perspective, I feel like I don’t know that much about this thing called "fine dust." People keep talking about it constantly, but I have many questions. First of all, as the name "fine dust" suggests, it isn't something that is immediately felt.

Aren't there known symptoms for when fine dust levels are severe? A stinging sensation in the parts of my body responsible for breathing? Because it persists, it feels like my insides are burning. My skepticism about people simply calling this "fine dust" is this: Is it really okay to just lump everything together and call it "fine dust"? Something like that.

Just based on this dust issue alone, I can feel that something is seriously wrong with the current situation. Why is that? Perhaps because I consider myself a living organism, an animal. As a living creature, the fact that even the basic act of breathing is this difficult feels like a grave matter. Just watching how people describe fine dust is frustrating. It’s as if they view the dust concentration as a specific stat in a video game. They seem to view the principles of why it rises and falls far too simplistically. This thought comes to mind:

In my view, the biggest reason for the increasing number of days with high fine dust is that the human population is simply too large. Yet, people today seem preoccupied with factional battles and headcount wars. They appear hell-bent on increasing their own faction's numbers just to crush those they’ve labeled as enemies. That’s how it looks to me, but the "solutions" people spout—like "let's save energy"—seem pointless. To sustain a population this size, no matter how minimally we act, fine dust is an inevitable outcome. Furthermore, the act of restraining human behavior must also be done by humans, which seems to inevitably lead to more conflict.

I recently saw the entry for "Overkill" on Namuwiki, which contained the following:

"It originated from 'excessive killing' in warfare—sacrificing too many enemies. During the Cold War, it meant possessing nuclear strike capabilities beyond what was necessary to destroy the enemy, resulting in the ability to completely crush the enemy nation or its facilities. The problem was that during the Cold War, the enemy would also strive to surpass the other's nuclear power, leading to an infinite loop and the realization of Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD)."

Reading this, I felt a connection. I thought that if you replaced "weapons" in that sentence with "human headcount," it would be just as valid. To me, the population is clearly not small, yet so many people are making a fuss, claiming it is. Far from being small, there are too many people even in my immediate surroundings. I wonder how massive the population must be that we can't accommodate everyone without building these high-rise buildings. I think the population density is nonsensical.

I feel that this fine dust situation is evidence that the human population is excessive. Do people know this or not? It’s true that this perspective isn't the mainstream one. It’s not that I don’t understand why people still claim the population is too small. I believe the reason is this: "Humans cannot distinguish between what is a mere expression and what is not." I know they say those things about headcounts because of things like "the nation" or "the economy." People immersed in those concepts think that without this many people, things won't function "normally"—or at least, that’s how I chose to think about it. To me, "economy" and "nation" are elements where the realm of "expression" is very strong. To put it differently, people talking about the economy or the nation looks no different to me than tracking specific stats in a video game.
