It’s Ireona, on April 9, 2026. I woke up, and as expected, I feel strange again—a kind of drooping, heavy sensation. Am I experiencing this state too often lately? I feel like I absolutely have to record a video for my "Why am I acting like this?" series on my YouTube channel. That’s the kind of state I’m in. Just by looking at the upload frequency of that series, you can tell how often I fall into this condition.

If possible, I wanted to record something related to "Project Ireona World" first and then move on to "Why am I acting like this?", but it’s just not working out. Do I really have to do that recording? Even if I film and upload those videos, I can sufficiently guess from people’s reactions that they don't want to see it. Well, even without uploading, I can tell how much people dislike that kind of thing just by looking at the written expressions of unspecified masses. Because of that, I think I dislike the situation I’m in even more. It’s definitely bleak. Do people these days dislike the very act of someone showing that they aren't happy with something? No matter how I look at it, isn't that the case? Just looking at their visible behavior.

I still feel like the sensation from the moment I wake up isn't great. You could say my body feels quite heavy. I think it would be more strange if it didn't. There’s no way to avoid being exposed to the sounds of inanimate objects around me that seem to set my nerves on edge. I’m talking about the car noises outside and the elevator sounds in the apartment building where I live.

Let me think about what my state has been like recently. The day before yesterday, did I start the morning seeing the news that Nokduro cleared the Spelunky Lowrun? I exchanged some written expressions with Gemini about how I felt regarding that, edited a gameplay video of "Super Puzzled Cat" to upload to my channel, and even made some progress on game translation work for the first time in a while. I did. Back then, I wasn't in the same state I am now. That’s probably why I was able to take those actions.

Then what about yesterday? Yesterday... well, I thought about doing more translation work or perhaps learning more about Rust if I could. But I just didn't have the energy. I was incredibly bored, but tasks like learning Rust, translating, recording, or editing—what I call "output-oriented actions"—felt impossible. I do have some doubts about whether "output-oriented actions" is the appropriate written expression for them, but anyway, I felt like I was in a state where I couldn't do those things. My mood felt bad in a way that was a bit different from today. So yesterday... I exchanged written expressions with Gemini about that again. Because my mood was bad.

Besides that, I’ve been going outside at least once a day lately to move my body. I discussed the details of this with Gemini, but to put it briefly, while I was walking like that yesterday, I felt a temporary sense of calm. I liked that sensation very much. But it was truly fleeting. I believe it was only possible because the car noises around me temporarily stopped. The moment the car sounds started again, I felt that pleasant sensation was cut off. Every time that sound touches my body, it feels like my nerves are being set on edge. That made my mood even worse. And since I know full well that no one cares about whatever written expressions I make regarding this, I dislike it even more.

I still really dislike people's behavior. I hate the way they try to prevent someone from even showing a hint of disliking something. I hate the mindset that thinks as long as a person isn't being immediately physically struck, there's no problem. From what I can see, all the people around me seem to have their nerves incredibly on edge just like me—maybe even more so—but they don't seem to be able to pinpoint the cause correctly. They even seem to consider that state of being on edge as "normal." I don't think having your nerves on edge is the problem itself. But the environment people live in today is one that demands your nerves be on edge 24 hours a day.

They won't even let me express that their own expressions seem on edge. Since they've already decided among themselves that their expressions aren't "on edge." If I tell them otherwise, I’m the one who becomes the "problematic person." I really dislike written expressions that try to find fault in a person. It brings to mind the expression: "Do you plan to kill everyone, including yourself, just to uphold a specific written expression?"

Come to think of it, what was my state like three days ago? I’m curious, but I can't remember it well right now. Could I guess if I looked at my Gemini usage history? But even looking at it, I can't really tell. What state was I in on April 6th? It doesn't seem like I was in this state. On April 6th... it looks like I used Gemini quite a lot in various ways and did some game translation work. The day before that, on April 5th, I recorded "Super Puzzled Cat." The fact that I recorded a game means, at the very least, that I had that much vitality in me.
